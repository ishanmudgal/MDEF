# **Situated Design Practices**

## Day 1

OCARINAS, OPPRESSION AND THE FARMS

From before the programme, I always wanted to build musical instruments. In fact, I had originally planned to make my project entirely about that. Instead I chose a more political concept, looking at oppression around the world. This field trip truned out to be a fascinating coming together of all these things. We learned about the oppression of the original inhabitants of the area who were mostly farmers, their land taken away to build an airport and other industrial and corporate ventures. Meeting the farmer and experienceing the collection of old objects and the in-progress museum were a great learning experience for me. I feel such experiuences are hidden away in plain sight in the city of Barcelona, and takes stepping off the paved roads to truly understand, and reveal such stories. Often while walking the streets of Barcelona I see the ornate decorations of the building there. It is devastatingly beautiful. Mainly because of my clear awareness of it being built from money brought in from colonies centuries ago. In my eyes it is built on the blood sweat and tears of a nation somewhere that is the opposite. A nation that is still struggling to keep up, and move beyond the third-world country tag. So this experience let me see SPain as more than just a coloniser, and realise the hidden oppression within the nation, that is true for almost any governance in the world today.

The second part which for me was absolutely incredible was the making of Ocarinas. Getting my hands dirty and building something had been very central for me at the start of this course. To play with clay and make an instrument was almost a dream. To get my Ocarina working took an incredible amount of patience, as it was very frustrating. the tiniest push or pull would stop any sound, or bring the sound back, and to finetune it was a pain. Finally it worked great, and it was a good learning because I got to make another Ocarina later in Senegal with the clay from the local swamps and shells, and got that working too! I feel I will certainly want to build more instruments and work closely with music, and this experience allowed me to understand that well.

## Day 2

SUPERCOMPUTING & THE CHURCH

One of the most surreal experiences I've had in years, the photon based computer inside a cathedral building was an awe-inspiring and chaotc experience for me. The reclaiming of a religious space for technology was strange too look at but also aesthetically stunning. I felt like I had entered an alternate reality or a sci-fi movie where the basic nature of the world and society was entirely different.

## Day 3

LIFE AS A SEX WORKER

Meeting and talking to the sex worker was an eye-opening and emotional experience for me. Firstly, learning about her daily life on the job and how she moved it from something that was more oppressive in nature towards her body, towards being a dominatrix - something that was more to her liking and allowed her to do the things she wanted while protecting herself was lovely to hear about. Second, realising that the issues she faced were not what I though they would be, was a revelation. It is easy to assume what such a life is like from outside based on a few stories, but truly every life and life experience is unique and can only be understood from the source, and a heart-to-heart sharing session ... maybe. During the question answers I had a lot of question to ask and the answers again were nothing I had expected. It was also a little difficult for me as I revealed a lot about myself as I asked these questions and that made me a bit conscious and anxious, but her calm energy and honest straight-forward answers made me realise life is a struggle, but one that must be faced, yet we do not have to face it with only negative energy, but with hope moving forward, the warmth of a light at the end of our tunnels.
