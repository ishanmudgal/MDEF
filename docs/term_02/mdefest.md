## My Reflections

The MDEFest for me was a difficult and nerve-wracking experience. My final work was a collection of tools, mostly digital with a few artifacts to show. The artifacts were also not physical, and had digital interactions. I struggled to figure out how to bring these together and got some great feedback during the class that really helped. I explored LoRa radio format to try and build alternative connected radio hubs but the code was complex and LoRa does not support complex audio transmissions very well, leading to a roadblock. At first I wanted to use it as a system to send messages between radio nodes, but the complexity outweighed the result, leading me to work on a different part from my collection instead of the radio.

I decided to work on multiple of my tools, and built a political weather map, an archive, and a coded comms game digitally. The process was relatively smooth (with help from AI). The basics were built using V0 AI for building websites, following which I edited the content and code to make it usable and interactive for my audiences. 

Before the MDEFest I spoke to many friends and other connections about my archive, its potential, as well as the political weather map. Many of them helped contribute to the map, adding their political views onto the map, helping it start off. During the MDEFest, I interacted with my audience closely, and we collectively went through a few entries on the map while choosing to keep them or remove them together.

Although people were very tired after a long day in the sun and many other project activations and noise from the afterparty, they interacted and shared their views, and also added political pinpoints on the map. It was great to receive feedback from people. They felt it had potential, and they would like to be a part of this and add to the collective map and access the archive in the future.

I feel the main issues from my end were that my activation was not very well-planned (and the noise from the party and overall audience energy levels also derailed me a bit), the products were all at a very initial stage and I was not able to bring out and showcase the connected power of a collection of tools as I had proposed. I feel I should have relied more on the MDEFest classes and faculty for feedback and to help my process, as well as my peers. I also should have created a connected performance, almost like a game that people would play with the cards to secretly communicate while trying to topple a system of control. It would have used the radio, map and secret comms platforms, while being more immersive and interesting.
